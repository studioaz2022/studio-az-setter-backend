**The 3 Kings**

Most impactful Onsite Placeholders:

- URL
- SEO Title Tag <title>
- Page Title H1
- Then after that is Subheadings [H2, H3…] > Content

URL Optimization

- 3-4 words max in the URL slug
- Do not repeat words. ex. “[zetaclearpros.com/zetaclear-review](http://zetaclearpros.com/zetaclear-review)”

- It hurts using target links with anchor texts

- Get the frequently repeating SINGLE WORDS from your top KW into the slug

SEO Title Tag Optimization 

- Try to get each single word in there (aim for 60-64 characters)
- Keep the top phrases TOGETHER and towards the front
- Write in english

- What would people say in a real sentence. Avoid: “have you found a good barbershop minneapolis recently”

- Do not repeat words
- Leave click bait until later

Page Title Optimization 

- Same rules as SEO Title
- Don’t make the SEO title and Page Title (H1) the same.

- Scramble them

**Featured Snippets (Position Zero)**

How to steal the featured snippet for question-based keywords (FAQ, Aftercare, informational pages):

1. Identify which target keywords have featured snippets in Google results
2. Match the structure of the current snippet — if it's a list, use a list. If it's a table, use a table. If it's a paragraph, use a paragraph
3. Precede the answer with an H2 that contains the query (e.g., `<h2>How Long Does a Tattoo Take to Heal?</h2>`)
4. Insert an optimized image near the snippet-targeted section
5. Use Google synonyms and related terms in the answer
6. Give a direct, concise answer immediately after the H2 — then expand with detail
7. NLP-friendly format: echo the question, give the answer, mention correct units/timeframes

Best pages for snippet targeting:
- FAQ page (every Q&A is a snippet opportunity)
- Aftercare page (healing timeline, care instructions)
- Services page (pricing questions, process questions)


**Getting The Visitor To Stay On Your Page (CRO)**

The Glance Test
- Load your page, look away, glance at it for 1 second, look away again
- In that 1 second, did you know exactly what the page is about?
- Use featured images, clear headlines, and prominent branding to pass the glance test
- Pages with featured images increase engagement by ~11%

Above the Fold Goals (in order):
1. Make the visitor feel they're in the right place (hero image + keyword-rich H1)
2. Build trust (professional design, credibility indicators, review count)
3. Hook them to keep reading (compelling first paragraph)

The Hook (First Paragraph)
- Three approaches:
  - **Knowledge bomb:** Hit with a surprising fact ("Minneapolis has over 40 tattoo shops — here's why artists choose Studio AZ")
  - **The entertainer:** Write in a relatable, conversational voice that connects emotionally
  - **Fear factor:** Create urgency ("Choosing the wrong shop can mean a tattoo you regret for life")
- Test which hook style works best for each page

Keeping Visitors Reading
- Write in short, bite-sized paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- Never have a full screen of text without a visual break (image, heading, table, callout)
- Use subheadings (H2/H3) to let skimmers jump to the section they want
- Use formatted content: tables, lists, callout boxes, expert quotes
- Use bucket brigades (cliffhangers): "more on this below," "as you'll see in a moment," "here's where it gets interesting"

The Sale Cycle — Match Content to Buyer Stage
- **Early stage** (informational): "does getting a tattoo hurt," "tattoo aftercare" → educate, don't pitch. Link them toward service/artist pages
- **Mid stage** (consideration): "best tattoo shop Minneapolis," "custom tattoo Minneapolis" → show proof, reviews, portfolio. CTA = "View Our Artists"
- **Late stage** (ready to act): artist pages, services page → direct CTA = "Book a Consultation"
- Don't push a late-stage CTA on an early-stage visitor (don't put "Book Now" on the aftercare page)

CTA Best Practices
- Use clear, actionable language: "Book a Free Consultation" beats "Learn More"
- "Learn More" is the worst CTA — visitors already did a lot of learning, don't ask for more
- Choose a CTA color that contrasts with your site's color scheme
- Put a CTA at the bottom of every page (matched to the page's buyer stage)
- On late-stage pages (artist pages), sprinkle 2-3 inline CTAs throughout the content
- On early-stage pages (aftercare, FAQ), use softer CTAs that move them to the next stage ("See Our Artists" or "View Our Portfolio")


**Website Maintenance (Post-Launch)**

Fresh Content Algorithm
- Google rewards sites that are continuously updated with new content
- **Bare minimum:** once per month, do two things:
  1. Add at least 1 new page or post (blog post, style guide, etc.)
  2. Update an existing piece of content (add a sentence, swap an image, add a video, update a testimonial)
- When adding new content, link it to relevant existing pages to build topical relevance
- Syndicate new content to social profiles (Instagram, Facebook)
- Watch for topic duplication — don't create a new page that overlaps with an existing one

Quarterly Keyword Audit
- Every 3 months, export current rankings from SerpAPI and compare to previous quarter
- Any keywords that are stagnant or dropped = candidates for content refresh
- Look for keywords we accidentally rank for but haven't optimized for — then optimize
- Three types of keyword finds:
  1. Keywords we rank for but don't have a page for → create a new page
  2. Keywords we rank for but the word isn't in our content → add it
  3. Keywords we rank for but the word isn't mentioned enough → increase frequency

Content Refresh Checklist (for stagnant/dropping pages)
- Is the content still accurate and up to date?
- Is word count still competitive? (check top 3 competitors, add 10-20%)
- Re-run TF*IDF — are niche keyword densities still optimal?
- Are there new subtopics that competitors cover that we don't?
- Updating content triggers Google to retest the page at a higher position
